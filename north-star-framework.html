Strategic Context — Why Now?

▶
  • 📈
    Teams adoption is accelerating
    Parent accounts represent our fastest-growing segment, but reporting gaps are creating churn risk and limiting upsell potential.
  • ⚡
    Competitive pressure is increasing
    HubSpot, Mailchimp, and Klaviyo all ship consolidated multi-account reporting. We're losing deals because prospects expect this baseline capability.
  • 🔧
    Infrastructure is ready
    Report Builder is live and stable. We have the foundation to build on — no longer blocked by legacy system constraints.
3

Problem / Opportunity

▶

User Scenario: Sarah manages 23 franchise locations. Every Monday, she spends 3+ hours impersonating each child account, manually exporting campaign data into spreadsheets, and trying to identify which locations need attention. By the time she's done, the data feels stale, errors have crept in, and she's no closer to strategic decisions. She's told us: "I need to see everything in one place, or I can't do my job."

  • 🚫
    No cross-account visibility
    Parent-level users cannot easily analyze performance across child accounts. There is no consolidated view for strategic decision-making. Impact: 68% of parent users report this as their #1 pain point (Q4 2025 survey).
  • 🔄
    Painful manual workaround
    Current process: impersonate child accounts, export CSVs, manually aggregate — slow, error-prone, and unscalable. Impact: Average 2.5 hours/week per parent user on manual reporting (CS data).
  • 📈
    Blocked enterprise growth
    Lack of parent-level reporting reduces enterprise adoption and strategic oversight, limiting multi-location expansion. Impact: Lost 4 enterprise deals in Q4 2025 explicitly due to reporting gaps (Sales feedback).

Milestones + AI Evolution

▶

Each milestone adds a channel + evolving AI capabilities. Order is driven by user pain, data readiness, and business impact.

Why This Order?
  • → Email first: Highest volume, most mature data model, biggest pain point (87% of parent users cite email reporting as critical need)
  • → Contact Growth second: Natural adjacency to email, enables audience health insights that drive retention conversations
  • → SMS/Social in parallel: Both channels have simpler data models and lower adoption — bundling accelerates delivery without sacrificing quality
  • → Automations deferred: Most complex data model, lower urgency, dependent on workflow taxonomy standardization (in progress)

Business Impact

▶
Gross Adds Impact
Parent-level reporting removes a key blocker in the enterprise sales cycle.
  • → Deal-closing feature: Eliminates impersonation as the "reporting story" in demos, replacing it with a consolidated dashboard that sells itself.
  • → Competitive parity: Closes the gap with HubSpot/Mailchimp who already offer multi-account reporting, removing a reason prospects walk.
  • → New selling motion: "Manage all locations from one dashboard" unlocks net-new parent account acquisition in multi-location verticals.
ASP Growth Impact
Reporting visibility creates natural upsell and larger deal sizes.
  • → Seat expansion: Parent accounts spot underperforming child accounts and add seats/channels to improve results — organic upsell.
  • → Higher contract values: Roll-up reporting proves portfolio-level ROI, justifying premium pricing and longer commitments.
  • → Multi-channel packaging: Phases 3-5 (SMS, Social, Automations) give sales ammunition to bundle more channels into deals, increasing ASP.

Risks & Dependencies

▶
Technical Risks
  • ⚠ Query performance at scale: Parent accounts with 50+ child accounts could hit query limits. Mitigation: pre-aggregation layer + caching strategy (in design).
  • ⚠ Data consistency: Cross-account rollups require eventual consistency. Mitigation: Clear "last updated" timestamps + manual refresh option.
Dependencies
  • 🔗 Report Builder stability: Phase 1 depends on Report Builder remaining stable and scalable. Engineering confirms ready for Teams integration.
  • 🔗 Permissions infrastructure: Requires parent/child role enforcement across data layer. Security team reviewing (expected approval by end of Q1).
  • 🔗 Automation taxonomy: Phase 5 (Automations) blocked until workflow naming standardization completes (currently Q3 2026).
10

Anti-Goals (Explicit Non-Goals)

▶
  • ✕ Build a full BI / analytics platform — we aggregate and surface, not replace Looker/Tableau
  • ✕ Real-time dashboards — near-real-time (hourly/daily refresh) is sufficient for v1
  • ✕ Child-account-level feature parity — parent view is strategic, not operational
  • ✕ Custom report builder from scratch — leverage existing Report Builder infrastructure
  • ✕ Cross-org benchmarking — comparing between unrelated parent accounts is out of scope
